## 1. Purpose and Scope

This doctrine governs the commissioned bounded membership access model; it does not supply market facts or token-economics policy.

Unsupported market prevalence is not product backing, and identifiable wallet balances are not a product-status lever.

## 2. Access Primitive

Threshold token holding unlocks the bounded membership channels.

## 3. Membership Shape

Membership supports holder, contributor, and core levels rather than a single undifferentiated gate.

## 4. Evidence-Bounded Outcome

Within the accepted bounded material, token gating is associated with improved member retention; this is not an external generalization.

## 5. Referral Loop

Gated perks form the intended referral loop.

## 6. Open Questions and Tensions

Which token architecture should implement the levels, whether a free tier should coexist with the gate, and whether referral rewards use tokens or points all remain open decisions.

## 7. Evidence Gaps

The accepted Precis supplies no externally validated retention effect size or success threshold.

## 8. Change Conditions

New external evidence or an authority decision requires a new accepted distillation or projection rather than a silent edit.
