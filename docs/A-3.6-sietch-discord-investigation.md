# A-3.6 — sietch-discord consumer investigation

**Conclusion: sietch-discord is NOT a Hasura consumer. The 5 synthesized fixtures must be removed.**

This document records the search performed to locate sietch-discord's production
GraphQL queries against envio's Hasura endpoint, and the affirmative finding that
no such consumer exists.

## What we were looking for

A-3 (PR #44 / 91d47bd) shipped `test/hasura-contract/fixtures/queries.json` with
15 entries — 5 per consumer × 3 consumers (freeside-score · mediums ·
sietch-discord). The 5 sietch-discord entries were marked `"synthesized": true`
with a `_meta.consumers.sietch-discord.real_source = "NOT FOUND in local
checkouts"` flag, deferring the resolution to A-3.6 (this document).

This investigation set out to resolve one of three outcomes:

1. Find sietch-discord's real consumer code and replace the 5 fixtures with real ones.
2. Find sietch-discord on Railway and lift queries from production logs.
3. Affirmatively determine sietch-discord is not a Hasura consumer and remove the fakes.

Outcome 3 is the one that landed.

## Architectural rationale

The "sietch-discord is a Hasura consumer" assumption appears to have originated
from the migration-plan vocabulary of "envelope subjects" (mintDetected, etc.)
which sietch reacts to **via NATS pub/sub**, not via direct Hasura reads. The
flow is:

```
envio (indexer) → outbox.publishEnvelope → NATS → sietch-discord subscribes
                                              ↓
                                       discord embed renderer
```

When sietch-discord needs to enrich a notification (e.g., "what's the latest
holder of this NFT?"), it reads either:
- The local sietch Postgres (where it persists its own derived state via drizzle), OR
- The chain directly via viem (`createPublicClient` / `readContract`).

It **never** queries the envio Hasura. That responsibility belongs to:
- `freeside-score` (score-mibera/trigger/utils/envio-client.ts — 5 real queries, retained)
- mediums (cubquests/medium-blink — 1 real query + 4 synthesized, retained pending
  separate validation — out of A-3.6 scope per operator brief)

## Resolution

The 5 sietch-discord entries in `test/hasura-contract/fixtures/queries.json`
must be **deleted entirely**, NOT replaced with real fixtures.

Asserting tests against fake queries would:
- Anchor a phantom contract that no real consumer relies on.
- Mask future drift: if sietch-discord ever DID start consuming Hasura, the test
  suite would already "pass" on a synthesized shape that may not match the real
  one.
- Mislead operators reading the runbook into thinking sietch-discord's
  read-path validates pre-cutover.

The fixtures count drops from 15 → 10 (5 freeside-score + 5 mediums).

The `_meta.consumers` block updates to remove `sietch-discord` and add a clear
"intentionally absent" note pointing at this investigation document.

## Implication for A-4 production cutover

Sietch-discord's correctness post-cutover is **independent of the Hasura
metadata flip**. Its data path goes:
- chain reads → unchanged (RPC URLs unaffected by Hasura cutover)
- local Postgres → unchanged (sietch-owned DB, separate from envio's)
- NATS subscriptions → unchanged (subjects are the envelope contract, not the
  Hasura schema)

The A-4 cutover validation can drop sietch-discord from the "consumer reconnect
drill" checklist. The drill still needs to validate:
- freeside-score (real Hasura consumer — 5 real queries).
- mediums (1 real + 4 synthesized; the 4 synthesized are flagged for separate
  validation per the operator brief).
